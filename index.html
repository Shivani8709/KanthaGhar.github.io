The stich magic

In modern usage, kantha more generally refers specifically to the type of stitch used. The earliest and most basic kantha stitch is a simple, straight, running stitch, like the type used on our Kantha Sari Scarves.Over time, more elaborate patterns developed, which became known as "nakshi kantha". Nakshi comes from the Bengali word, naksha, which refers to artistic patterns. Nakshi kantha is made up of motifs influenced by religion, culture and the lives of the women stitching them.

Rags to riches stories

On a wall at India International Centre, textile art researcher Jasleen Dhamija draws attention to a Baul song that cites one of India's oldest types of embroidery, the Kantha. The best examples of this complex needlework produced by West Bengal women on small pieces of cloth portray thumbnails of children staring at animals and acrobats hanging in the air at a circus, as well as life near Shantiniketan's train station..

Simplest stitch in the language of embroidery

Kantha comprises of the simplest stitch in the language of embroidery – the running stitch. It is the way in which this stitch is used, in different arrangements, that forms the complex vocabulary of kantha.

House of Wandering Silk

As with all traditional textiles, kantha was influenced by external factors such as material availability, daily needs, climate, geography and economic factors. Historically, textile production was one of the most labour-intensive industries, and as such, textiles were highly valued. Thus, the recycling of well used cloth-turned-rags was a natural step in the lifecycle of textiles the world over. Given that this recycling was home-based work, it usually fell to the women of the village to prepare, cut and stitch the rags - giving old textiles new life.

The chisel meets the needle

Kolkata born Meera Mukherjee (1923-1998), led an unconventional life, studying in Kolkata and New Delhi. She then went on to study at Munich, where she learnt and worked under some of the best artistes of the world. It was here that Toni Stadler encouraged her transition from painter to sculptor. This eventually helped her create a paradigm of modernistic works using traditional Indian crafting techniques. Her book, In search of Viswakarma, can be called a culmination of her research and studies of traditional Indian arts and crafts.

Kantha Stitch: The Evolution from Old to New

Activists from around the world have been working tirelessly to bring back the true essence of Kantha. Known as a revolutionary and teacher, Shamlu Dudeja first emphasized the prominence of this craft. She urged women to take this art form more seriously as a career option. She laid the groundwork for Kantha embroidery, which became so popular in the years following her death. It was Kantha embroidery that provided work for women. It is empowering women to become self-sufficient entrepreneurs. Kantha has been given a modern twist by a number of designers, including Tarun Thaliani's summer/resort 2013 collection. Many craft inventions and designers have worked on kantha, making it more than just a household item. Today's innovative products target niche markets by incorporating rich fabrics into their embroideries, which was previously unheard of. Kantha is used in a wide variety of products today, including cushions, silk saris, jackets, pants, and other home furnishings. It's becoming more and more commercialized, and the craft is finding a new place in people's hearts.
